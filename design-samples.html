Narrative Systems

Tarot Card Combat

In Eidolon: Fate of Fools, the hook hinges on the ludonarrative integration; what does it mean to play a tarot card arena shooter? I designed over a dozen tarot-inspired weapons to incorporate traditional symbolism through play.

Each mystical weapon is a tarot card with two states: upright and inverted. Upright tarot abilities resemble familiar shooter weapons, including limited ammunition per card. Inverted abilities always discard the card instantly for a more unique effect - sometimes even affecting other cards.

Dynamic Critter Encounters

In Cats & Critters, a multiplayer party of cat wizards adventure through an archipelago filled with mischievous critters! By extending the response curve functionality from the utility AI system I built, I created a dynamic encounter system which reacts to various gameplay statistics.

This allowed integrating a variety of encounters including ambushes, wave challenges, and a three-phase final boss fight - all of which include dynamic scaling tailored to the party. This encounter system also proved important for progression gating the three islands and the final arena.

The Cold Case Codebase

The first game jam I participated in featured the theme of "text-only." As a writer, I naturally set out to make some unique flavor of interactive fiction. And as the sole programmer, I went on a proverbial frenzy implementing mechanics for my cheeky detective game: The Chartide Cases.

Writing hundreds of dialogue assets was only half the battle, since I also implemented all of these features:

  • A mock command-line interface and parser with contextual TAB autocomplete
  • The applauded "recall" mechanic which allows asking any character about any entity in the game (or jogging your own memory)
  • Branching dialogue choices and progress flags which trickle into 7 different endings
  • Color-coded text to signify when progress flags are met and which interactions may have updated because of it
  • A typewriter effect for all dialogue including variable speeds to emphasize speech inflection
  • A custom script editor to manage all of the written assets and their support for all of these features

There only remains one mystery: is it possible to recover the project source?

Setting

Whimsical Storybook Sci-Fi

As the first world explored in SAROS, Erylliad is a planet which must accentuate a pivot from familiarity to otherworldliness. In tune with the serendipitous arrival of Cosmo, a girl from the stars, the land itself reacts to the abundance of starlight - morphing and revealing ancient secrets left by an absent culture of astronomers.

Character

Whimsical Storybook Sci-Fi

Atlas is the deuteragonist of SAROS: a kid astronaut who promises to accompany a lost Celesti girl on her journey back to the opposite end of the universe. He is an optimistic yet brash young teenager with a longing for his lost family and an affinity for adventure. Mechanically, Atlas embraces recklessness and destruction where Cosmo utilizes caution and creation.
